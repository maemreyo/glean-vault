> [!info] Word Analysis
> **IPA:** /EËˆ5:0rV fr9m/
> **Part of Speech:** prepositional phrase
> **CEFR Level:** B2
> **Register:** neutral/formal
> **Etymology:** From "apart" (separately) + "from" (indicating exclusion)

> [!abstract] Meanings
> **Root Meaning:** Except for; with the exclusion of
>
> **Context Meaning:** Used to introduce an exception to a statement or to indicate that someone/something is not included in what was just mentioned
>
> **Vietnamese Translation:**
> - **Direct:** ngoai tru, tru ra
> - **Contextual:** ben canh (doi voi), ke ca
>
> **Nuances:**
> - Can mean "except for" (exclusion) or "besides" (addition) depending on context
> - Often used to introduce an additional point or exception

> [!tip] Word Vibe & Story
> **Personality:** Flexible, Contextual
> **Memory Hook:** Think of "apart" as "set aside from" - you're setting something aside from the group
> **Etymology Story:** From Old French "a part" meaning "to one side" - literally putting something to the side, separate from the main group

> [!note] Relations
> **Word Family:**
> - **Phrase:** [[Apart from]] /EËˆ5:0rV fr9m/
> - **Related:** [[apart]] /EËˆ5:0rVt/
> - **Related:** [[except]] /9kËˆsept/
>
> **Synonyms:**
> - [[except for]] /9kËˆsept fEËr/ : direct replacement
> - [[besides]] /b9Ëˆsa9dz/ : when adding information
> - [[other than]] /909r 0Pn/ : formal alternative
>
> **Antonyms:**
> - [[including]] /9nËˆkluËd9N/ : opposite function
> - [[especially]] /9ËˆspeSli/ : highlighting instead of excluding

> [!success] Context & Usage
> **Collocations:**
> 1. apart from that
> 2. apart from this
> 3. apart from the fact that
>
> **1. Example Context 1 (Exclusion)**
> - **Sentence:** The project was successful apart from a few minor delays.
> - **Translation:** Du an thanh cong tru mot so cham tre nho.
>
> **2. Example Context 2 (Addition)**
> - **Sentence:** Apart from being a talented musician, she's also a gifted writer.
> - **Translation:** ngoai viec la mot nha si tai ba, con la mot nha van tai nang.

> [!failure] Common Mistakes
> **1. Confusion with "except"**
> - **Mistake:** Everyone came to the party apart John.
> - **Correction:** Everyone came to the party apart from John. / Everyone came to the party except John.
> - **Why:** "Apart from" requires "from" - it's a fixed phrase

> [!quote]- Context Source
> **Quote:** "Apart from the cost, the plan seems feasible."
