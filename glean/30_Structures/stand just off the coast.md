stand just off the coast
> [!info] Structural Analysis
> **Structure Type:** Phrasal Verb + Prepositional Phrase (Location Description)
>
> **Formula / Pattern:**
> ```text
> [Verb] + [Adverb] + [Preposition] + [Article] + [Noun]
> ```
>
> **Components:**
> - **Component 1:** Verb "stand" - to be in an upright position
> - **Component 2:** Adverb "just" - emphasizing proximity or exactness
> - **Component 3:** Preposition "off" - indicating separation from land
> - **Component 4:** Article "the" - specifying particular coast
> - **Component 5:** Noun "coast" - land beside sea
>
> **Formation Rules:**
> 1. Phrasal verb "stand off" means to remain at a distance
> 2. "Just" modifies "off" to emphasize minimal distance
> 3. Prepositional phrase specifies location relative to coast
> 4. No subject needed as this is an infinitive phrase structure

> [!abstract] Meaning & Usage
> **Core Meaning:**
> - **Literal:** To be positioned slightly away from the shoreline
> - **Figurative:** To remain separate but nearby from main action or group
>
> **Functional Meaning:**
> - **Purpose:** To describe precise geographical positioning
> - **Nuance:** Nautical/geographical register, exact spatial description
