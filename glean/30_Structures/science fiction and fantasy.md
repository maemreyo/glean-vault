> [!info] Structural Analysis
> **Structure Type:** Compound Noun Phrase / Genre Classification
>
> **Formula / Pattern:**
> ```text
> [genre 1] + and + [genre 2]
> ```
>
> **Components:**
> - **Component 1:** Compound noun "science fiction" - first genre
> - **Component 2:** Conjunction "and" - links parallel elements
> - **Component 3:** Noun "fantasy" - second genre
>
> **Formation Rules:**
> 1. "Science fiction" is a fixed compound noun (always two words)
> 2. "and" coordinates two parallel nouns of equal importance
> 3. No article needed when used as general category
> 4. Pattern extends to: [genre] + and + [genre]

> [!abstract] Meaning & Usage
> **Core Meaning:**
> - **Literal:** Two literary/film genres: one based on scientific principles, the other on magical elements
> - **Figurative:** Can represent imagination beyond reality, speculative storytelling, or escapism
>
> **Functional Meaning:**
> - **Purpose:** To categorize creative works that blend scientific and magical elements
> - **Nuance:** Often treated as a single combined category in publishing/media
