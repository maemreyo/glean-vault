> [!info] Structural Analysis
> **Structure Type:** Idiom (Expression of regret/disappointment)
>
> **Formula / Pattern:**
> ```text
> [That/It/What] + [was/is/will be] + a + [shame/pity/cry shame]
> ```
>
> **Components:**
> - **Introductory word:** That/It/What (referring to a situation)
> - **Linking verb:** was/is/will be (or would be in hypothetical)
> - **Article:** a (can sometimes be omitted with "what")
> - **Noun of disappointment:** shame/pity/cry shame
>
> **Formation Rules:**
> 1. "That" refers to specific past situation, "It" to current/general
> 2. "What a shame" is an exclamation (usually with exclamation mark)
> 3. Tense changes based on timing of regretted event
> 4. "Would be a shame" for hypothetical situations
> 5. Can be followed by "that" + clause: "It's a shame that you couldn't come"

> [!abstract] Meaning & Usage
> **Core Meaning:**
> - **Literal:** Something brings shame or disgrace
> - **Figurative:** Regrettable situation, unfortunate outcome, disappointing event
>
> **Functional Meaning:**
> - **Purpose:** To express sympathy, disappointment, or regret about a situation
> - **Nuance:** Empathetic response, can show support or shared disappointment
>
