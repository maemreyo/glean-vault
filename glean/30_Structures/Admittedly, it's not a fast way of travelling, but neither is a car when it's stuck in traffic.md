> [!info] Structural Analysis
> **Structure Type:** Pattern (Concession + Negative Parallel)
>
> **Formula / Pattern:**
> ```text
> Admittedly, + [Negative statement about A], + but + neither is/does + [B] + [condition]
> ```
>
> **Components:**
> - **Component 1:** "Admittedly," - Concession marker acknowledging a point
> - **Component 2:** Negative statement about the first item (A)
> - **Component 3:** "but" - Contrast conjunction
> - **Component 4:** "neither" + inverted auxiliary (is/does/can) + second item (B)
> - **Component 5:** Optional qualifying condition for B
>
> **Formation Rules:**
> 1. Start with "Admittedly," followed by a comma
> 2. State a negative fact or limitation about the first subject
> 3. Use "but" to introduce contrast
> 4. Use "neither" with inverted auxiliary verb (subject-auxiliary inversion)
> 5. Add optional condition that contextualizes the comparison

> [!abstract] Meaning & Usage
> **Core Meaning:**
> - **Literal:** Acknowledging a negative quality of something while pointing out that another alternative has the same or worse quality
> - **Figurative:** Using concession to strengthen an argument by showing both options have similar flaws
>
> **Functional Meaning:**
> - **Purpose:** To make a balanced argument by acknowledging limitations of your preferred option
> - **Nuance:** Sophisticated rhetorical device, fair-minded, academic/formal register
>
> **Vietnamese Translation:**
> - **Direct:** "Thừa nhận rằng..., nhưng... cũng không..."
> - **Contextual:** "Đúng là..., nhưng... thì cũng vậy khi..."

> [!note] Relations & Connections
> **Synonyms / Similar Structures:**
> - [[it's true that]] /ɪts truː ðæt/ : similar concession marker
> - [[while it may be]] /waɪl ɪt meɪ biː/ : concessive clause structure
>
> **Antonyms / Contrasting Structures:**
> - [[not only but also]] /nɒt ˈəʊnli bʌt ˈɔːlsəʊ/ : addition instead of comparison
> - [[on the contrary]] /ɒn ðə ˈkɒntrəri/ : direct opposition

> [!success] Usage Examples
>
> **1. Formal Context**
> - **Sentence:** Admittedly, the proposal lacks detailed implementation, but neither does the competing alternative offer any concrete solutions.
> - **Translation:** Thừa nhận rằng đề xuất này thiếu chi tiết thực thi, nhưng giải pháp cạnh tranh cũng không đưa ra bất kỳ giải pháp cụ thể nào.
> - **Analysis:** Formal academic writing using balanced comparison to critique both options fairly.
>
> **2. Informal Context**
> - **Sentence:** Admittedly, this restaurant is a bit pricey, but neither is the fancy one downtown worth the extra money.
> - **Translation:** Đúng là nhà hàng này hơi đắt, nhưng nhà hàng sang trọng ở trung tâm cũng không đáng với số tiền thêm đó.
> - **Analysis:** Casual conversation comparing two expensive dining options.
>
> **3. Business/Academic Context**
> - **Sentence:** Admittedly, the new software requires extensive training, but neither can we afford to continue with our outdated legacy system.
> - **Translation:** Thừa nhận rằng phần mềm mới cần đào tạo kỹ lưỡng, nhưng chúng ta cũng không thể đủ sức chi trả để tiếp tục với hệ thống lỗi thời cũ kỹ.
> - **Analysis:** Business justification for change despite difficulties.

> [!failure] Common Mistakes
>
> **1. Pattern Error**
> - ❌ **Mistake:** Admittedly, it's not fast, but neither a car is...
> - ✅ **Correction:** Admittedly, it's not fast, but neither is a car...
> - **Why:** "Neither" requires subject-auxiliary inversion (auxiliary before subject).
>
> **2. Usage Error**
> - ❌ **Mistake:** Admittedly, it's fast, but neither is a car...
> - ✅ **Correction:** Admittedly, it's not fast, but neither is a car...
> - **Why:** The structure requires a negative statement about the first item for logical flow.

> [!tip] Learning Strategies
> - **Visual Association:** Picture a balanced scale with two drawbacks on either side
> - **Memory Hook:** "Admit the bad, show the other's bad too"
> - **Common Collocations:** admittedly, it's not, but neither is/does
> - **Related Structures:** [[grant that]] vs [[admittedly]]

## 🧩 Context Analysis (Advanced)

> [!example] Original Source
> **Quote:** "Admittedly, it's not a fast way of travelling, but neither is a car when it's stuck in traffic."
> **Context:** Speaker comparing bicycle travel to car travel, acknowledging bicycle's speed limitation while pointing out cars have the same problem in traffic.
> **Effectiveness:** Highly effective rhetorical strategy that preempts criticism by showing fair consideration of both options.

### Card 5: Error Correction

🕵️ **Spot the Error:** What's wrong with: "Admittedly, the movie was long, but neither it was boring"
?
> 🛠️ **Correction:** Admittedly, the movie was long, but neither was it boring.
> 🚫 **Trap:** "Neither" đòi hỏi đảo ngữ - trợ động từ lên trước chủ ngữ (was it thay vì it was)

### Card 6: Comparison (Nuance)

🆚 **Comparison:** How is **Admittedly, it's not a fast way of travelling, but neither is a car** different from [[Although it's not fast, a car isn't either]]?
?
> ⚖️ **Difference:** "Admittedly" shows active acknowledgment and intellectual honesty, while "Although" is a simple concessive connector
> 🧱 **The Barrier:** "Admittedly" thể hiện sự chủ động thừa nhận, tạo ấn tượng khách quan hơn là "Although" đơn thuần
