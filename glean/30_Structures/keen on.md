# üìä Keen On

## üîç Structural Analysis

### Pattern Recognition
**Structure Type:** Adjective + Preposition Combination
**Formula/Pattern:** [Subject] + be + keen + on + [Noun/Gerund/Pronoun]
**Components:**
- **Component 1:** [Subject] - Person expressing interest
- **Component 2:** be - Linking verb (conjugated: am/is/are/was/were)
- **Component 3:** keen - Adjective meaning eager/enthusiastic
- **Component 4:** on - Preposition linking to the object of interest
- **Component 5:** [Noun/Gerund/Pronoun] - What the person is interested in

### Formation Rules
**Rule 1:** The verb "be" must agree with the subject
**Rule 2:** Always use "on" after "keen" (fixed preposition)
**Rule 3:** After "on", use:
   - Noun: "keen on music"
   - Gerund (-ing): "keen on learning"
   - Pronoun: "keen on it"
**Rule 4:** Cannot use infinitive after "on" (‚ùå keen on to learn)

### Variations
1. **Variation A: Comparative Form**
   - **When to use:** Comparing levels of interest
   - **Example:** "She's keener on art than on science"

2. **Variation B: Question Form**
   - **When to use:** Asking about interests
   - **Example:** "Are you keen on joining us?"

3. **Variation C: Negative Form**
   - **When to use:** Expressing lack of interest
   - **Example:** "I'm not keen on horror movies"

## ‚ö†Ô∏è Common Mistakes

| Mistake ‚ùå | Correction ‚úÖ | Explanation |
| ----------- | ------------- | ----------- |
| "I'm keen on to learn Japanese" | "I'm keen on learning Japanese" | Use gerund (-ing), not infinitive after preposition |
| "She is keen in music" | "She is keen on music" | Fixed preposition - always use "on" after "keen" |
| "They keen on playing football" | "They are keen on playing football" | Need the verb "be" before adjective "keen" |
| "He is very keen on it very much" | "He is very keen on it" OR "He likes it very much" | Don't double intensifiers |

### Error Patterns
1. **Pattern Error: Wrong verb form after "on"**
   - **Common mistake:** Using infinitive instead of gerund
   - **Why it happens:** Direct translation from languages where infinitives are used
   - **How to avoid:** Remember: Preposition + Gerund (on + learning)

2. **Usage Error: Wrong preposition**
   - **Common mistake:** Using "in", "about", or "for" instead of "on"
   - **Why it happens:** Interference from other adjective-preposition pairs
   - **How to avoid:** "Keen" is always followed by "on" - it's a fixed collocation
