# üìä Formalize In

## üîç Structural Analysis

### Pattern Recognition
**Structure Type:** Verb + Preposition Combination
**Formula/Pattern:**
[Subject] + formalize(s) + [Object] + in + [Medium/Format/Writing]

**Components:**
- **Component 1:** [Subject] - Person/organization making something formal
- **Component 2:** formalize(s) - Main verb (to make formal)
- **Component 3:** [Object] - Agreement, relationship, or idea being formalized
- **Component 4:** in - Preposition indicating the medium of formalization
- **Component 5:** [Medium/Format/Writing] - The form in which something is made official

### Formation Rules
**Rule 1:** "Formalize" always requires an object (what is being formalized)
**Rule 2:** Preposition "in" indicates the medium or format of formalization
**Rule 3:** After "in", use:
   - Noun for medium: "formalized in writing"
   - Noun for document type: "formalized in a contract"
   - Noun for format: "formalized in a memorandum"
**Rule 4:** Often used in passive voice when focusing on the object

### Variations
1. **Variation A: Passive Voice (Common)**
   - **When to use:** When the result is more important than the actor
   - **Example:** "The agreement was formalized in a written contract"

2. **Variation B: Active Voice**
   - **When to use:** When emphasizing who is making something formal
   - **Example:** "The company formalized the policy in an official memo"

3. **Variation C: With "be/get"**
   - **When to use:** State or change of formality
   - **Example:** "Their understanding got formalized in a document"

## üí° Meaning & Usage

**Literal meaning:** To give formal or official status to something
**Figurative meaning:** To make explicit, official, or properly structured

### Functional Meaning
**Purpose in communication:** To indicate officialization, documentation, or establishment of formal status
**Intended effect:** To show legitimacy, official recognition, or proper procedure
**Semantic role:** Transitive verb of formalization/officialization

## ‚ö†Ô∏è Common Mistakes

| Mistake ‚ùå | Correction ‚úÖ | Explanation |
| ----------- | ------------- | ----------- |
| "They formalized their agreement" | "They formalized their agreement IN writing" | Missing preposition "in" and medium |
| "The policy formalized in verbally" | "The policy was formalized verbally" OR "The policy was formalized in words" | Wrong form and preposition usage |
| "Formalize in this decision" | "Formalize this decision IN a document" | Word order and missing medium |
| "We formalized with a contract" | "We formalized the agreement IN a contract" | Wrong preposition and missing object |

### Error Patterns
1. **Pattern Error: Missing Preposition**
   - **Common mistake:** Omitting "in" and the medium
   - **Why it happens:** Treating "formalize" as complete without specifying format
   - **How to avoid:** Always ask: "In what form is this being formalized?"

2. **Usage Error: Wrong Preposition**
   - **Common mistake:** Using "with" or "by" instead of "in"
   - **Why it happens:** Confusion with other verb-preposition combinations
   - **How to avoid:** Remember: "formalize IN [medium/document]"

## üìù Quick Reference

### Structure Formula
**Template:** [Subject] + formalize(s) + [Object] + in + [Medium/Document]
**Fill-in-the-blanks:** We will formalize the agreement in ___.

### Quick Rules
1. **Rule 1:** Always include object (what is being formalized)
2. **Rule 2:** Always use preposition "in" + medium/document
3. **Rule 3:** Specify the form of formalization (writing, contract, etc.)

### Dos and Don'ts
**‚úÖ Dos:**
- Use for making informal arrangements official
- Specify the medium or document type
- Use in legal, business, and formal contexts

**‚ùå Don'ts:**
- Don't use without specifying the medium
- Don't confuse with "formalize with" (different meaning)
- Don't use for simple, casual agreements

### Signal Words/Phrases
**Words that often indicate this structure:**
